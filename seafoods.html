    Top 6 Seafoods for a healthy lifeFRUITS

    Salmon

    Rich in Omega-3s & Protein Salmon is one of the best sources of omega-3 fatty acids, which promote heart health, reduce inflammation, and support brain function. Wild-caught salmon is especially nutritious.

    Sardine

    High in Calcium & Vitamin D These small fish are packed with calcium, vitamin D, and omega-3s, making them great for bone health and cardiovascular support. Plus, they are low in mercury compared to larger fish.

    Tuna

    Good for Muscle Growth & Brain Health Tuna is a high-protein fish rich in B vitamins, selenium, and omega-3s. However, it’s best to consume it in moderation due to potential mercury content, especially in larger tuna species.

    Shrimp

    Low-Calorie & High in Protein Shrimp is an excellent source of lean protein, selenium, and antioxidants like astaxanthin, which help reduce inflammation and support skin health.

    Oysters

    High in Zinc & Vitamin B12 Oysters are a powerhouse of zinc, iron, and vitamin B12, making them great for immune function, brain health, and skin repair. They are also rich in antioxidants.

    Crab

    Supports Joint & Heart Health Crab is high in protein, omega-3s, and vitamin B12, which contribute to strong bones, improved heart health, and better cognitive function

    Final Thoughts

    Eating a variety of seafood 2-3 times a week provides essential nutrients that promote a healthy heart, brain, and immune system. Choose wild-caught or sustainably farmed options whenever possible for the best nutritional benefits.
